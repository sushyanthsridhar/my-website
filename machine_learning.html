Vaccine Distribution Optimization: Overview

The objective was to deliver three types of vaccines to 200 nodes while minimizing the total travel distance. Key constraints included vaccine-specific delivery windows, cooling requirements, vehicle capacities, and fuel limits. The model used two approaches:

Gurobi Optimization:

  • Applied Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) to find the optimal route.
  • Incorporated subtour elimination constraints and time-dependent delivery requirements.
  • Ensured vaccines were delivered efficiently within fuel and capacity limits.

Greedy Heuristic:

  • Implemented a nearest-neighbor heuristic to assign routes quickly by choosing the closest unvisited node.
  • Offered a scalable solution for large datasets, focusing on speed and feasibility, though with slightly reduced precision compared to the Gurobi approach.
