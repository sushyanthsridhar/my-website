Dengue Control Study

Background

Dengue in Argentina: Dengue is transmitted by Aedes aegypti, a mosquito species native to tropical and subtropical climates.

Historical Spread: Before 2009, dengue cases were restricted to northern Argentina.

Recent Outbreak: During the 2023-2024 season, Argentina reported over 120,000 cases of dengue.

Location Focus: Córdoba, Argentina

Climate: Córdoba's warm and humid climate, with frequent rainfall, creates a favorable breeding environment for Aedes aegypti.

Healthcare Challenge: A surge in dengue-related deaths raised concerns about the healthcare system’s ability to handle such outbreaks.

Statistics

2023-2024 Outbreak in Córdoba:

  • Total Cases: 566,141
  • Dengue-related Deaths: 415
  • Hospitals Available: 2,500
  • Case-to-Hospital Ratio: 0.0044

Ovitrap Data

Data Collection Timeline: Egg counts were monitored at 1.5 km intervals from 2009 to 2013, with additional data in 2023-2024.

Observations: A positive correlation was found between temperature and egg counts, indicating that warmer temperatures enhanced mosquito breeding.

Forecasting: Predictive models were developed to fill gaps and extend projections through 2024.

Modeling and Simulations

Mosquito Lifecycle Modeling

Lifecycle Stages: The simulation follows four key life stages: egg, larva, pupa, and adult mosquito.

Development and Mortality Rates:

  • Egg: Mortality (0.25–0.5), Development (0.4–0.6)
  • Larva: Mortality (0.3–0.5), Development (0.5–0.7)
  • Pupa: Mortality (0.1–0.2), Development (0.87–0.97)

Environmental Variables: Temperature, diurnal range, and larval density are integrated into the model to assess lifecycle dynamics.

Simulation Results

Duration: Simulations ran for 780 weeks.

Key Findings:

  • Total Eggs: 33,877
  • Total Larvae: 22,619
  • Total Pupae: 15,906
  • Total Adults: 14,643
  • Mortality Rate (Egg to Adult): 43%

Goals of the Study

Forecasting: Estimate mosquito populations by ovitrap to predict trends for the 2025-2026 season.

Optimization: Use non-linear stochastic models to predict infections, accounting for neighborhood clustering, elevation, and accessibility.

Resource Allocation: Develop a strategy for the placement of testing and treatment centers at optimal locations.

Performance Enhancement: Apply heuristics to minimize the optimality gap in the solutions obtained.

Model Description

Compartments:

  • Mosquito: Divided into four life stages—eggs, larvae, pupae, and adults. Adult females are further classified as susceptible or infectious.
  • Human Population: Classified into five groups: susceptible, exposed, infectious (with DEN-1 to DEN-4 strains), and removed.

Integration: The mosquito-human interaction model incorporates disease transmission dynamics with stochastic resource allocation.

Machine Learning Results

Model Inputs:

  • X₁: Temperature
  • X₂: Diurnal Temperature Range
  • X₃: Larval Density

Coefficients: Multiple coefficients (e.g., B₁, B₂, Ba) were used to weigh the variables and determine their impact on outcomes.
