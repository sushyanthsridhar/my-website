Ganesh Chaturthi Environmental Study

Abstract

This paper addresses the environmental issues associated with the Ganesh Chaturthi festival in Mumbai, specifically focusing on pollution caused by idol immersion. The study proposes the use of a deterministic mixed-integer programming model and demand forecasting techniques to optimize idol production, with efforts to incorporate eco-friendly materials like terracotta. The analysis involves de-seasonalizing demand for idols over the past six years using the Box-Jenkins method to improve forecasting and resource management.

Introduction: Mumbai as a Case Study

Ganesh Chaturthi Festival: The festival attracts millions to Mumbai, posing logistical challenges due to overcrowded public spaces and transportation networks.

Pollution Issues: Increased waste generation, including plastic, food leftovers, and decorations.

Noise pollution from firecrackers and music affects both residents and wildlife.

Economic and Cultural Impact: Demand for idols surges before the festival, supporting local artisans. However, promoting eco-friendly practices is crucial for sustainable celebrations.

Water Pollution in Marine Drive

Environmental Impact of Idol Immersion: Plaster of Paris (PoP) idols release toxic chemicals, heavy metals, and dyes, harming aquatic ecosystems.

Organic offerings cause nutrient enrichment, contributing to eutrophication.

Debris accumulation affects marine organisms through ingestion and entanglement.

Solutions Proposed:

  • Strict regulations to ban non-biodegradable idols.
  • Awareness campaigns encouraging clay idols and natural dyes.
  • Use of immersion tanks and designated immersion sites to contain pollution.

Eco-Friendly Solutions

Clay (Terracotta) Idols: Biodegradable and eco-friendly, they release harmless minerals back into the water.

Plant-Based Materials: Utilizing agricultural waste (e.g., coconut husk, rice husks) supports sustainability and reduces waste.

Innovative Materials: Paper mache and natural resins offer lightweight, customizable options with minimal environmental impact.

Literature Review

Demand Forecasting

Methods: Time series models like ARIMA, exponential smoothing, and neural networks are common techniques.

Data Quality: Effective forecasting relies on integrating data from multiple sources, including social media and weather indicators.

Accuracy Metrics: Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Squared Error (MSE), and Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) are used to measure accuracy.

Challenges: Forecasting in volatile markets and supply chains with varying lead times and reliability remains complex.

Optimization Techniques

Linear and Mixed-Integer Programming (MIP): These frameworks optimize inventory and production schedules.

Integration with Forecasting Models: Time series methods guide inventory adjustments based on predicted demand.

Multi-Objective Optimization: Tools like genetic algorithms address trade-offs between cost minimization and customer satisfaction.

Real-Time Optimization

IoT and Real-Time Data: Companies use streaming data for dynamic pricing and inventory management.

Challenges: Data quality, model scalability, and cross-functional collaboration are crucial for effective optimization.

Seasonal Forecasting: Box-Jenkins Method

Overview: ARIMA models are effective for forecasting data with seasonal patterns.

Implementation: Moving averages smooth out noise, while exponential smoothing assigns weights to recent data points.

Evaluation: Diagnostic tools like the Ljung-Box test ensure model accuracy, with out-of-sample forecasting used for validation.

Methodology

Model Formulation: A deterministic mixed-integer programming model was implemented using Python and IBM CPLEX on a 13th Gen Intel Core i5 processor. The model integrated de-seasonalized demand data with resource constraints to optimize production schedules.

Results

Demand Forecasts: Predictive modeling highlighted fluctuations in idol orders, with projections provided for the next three years.

Optimization Impact: The use of eco-friendly materials and improved forecasting led to better resource management, minimizing environmental impact while supporting local economies.
